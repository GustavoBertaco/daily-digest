## 📺 AI Engineer

### [Stop Making Models Bigger, Make Them Behave — Kobie Crawford, Snorkel](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TNwJ1LMiENk)
*Published: June 10, 2026*

- Snorkel fine-tuned a 4B model with RL for under $500, and it outperformed Qwen 3 235B on a real-world SQL task — the smaller model inspected the schema first, self-corrected on a column error, and returned the right answer while the 235B model hallucinated after querying a non-existent table twice.
- The thesis: for tool-use tasks, tool discipline matters more than raw reasoning depth, and targeted RL training can instill that discipline in small models at a fraction of the cost of scaling.
- Counterintuitively, making models bigger does not improve agentic reliability — behavioral training on task-specific tool use does.

### [Sovereign Escape Velocity: Ownership with Open Models — Gus Martins & Ian Ballantyne, Google DeepMind](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SS-A8sE7hkw)
*Published: June 10, 2026*

- Gemma 4's 31B model ranks fourth on the LM Arena open model leaderboard while running on a single GPU — the competing models at comparable quality are at least twice as large, and some are 20 times larger.
- This size efficiency unlocks deployment scenarios that closed, large-scale models cannot reach: running on a Pixel phone (2–4B GPU memory despite more parameters), deploying a medical variant on two GPUs for an entire hospital, and running parallel instances.
- The key insight is that parameter efficiency, not parameter count, determines real-world deployment sovereignty — the models you can own and run are the ones that fit your infrastructure.

### [Self Driving Products: Product Signals to Pull Requests — Joshua Snyder, PostHog](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zMiSRliEzv4)
*Published: June 10, 2026*

- PostHog is building an agentic pipeline that collapses the entire developer triage loop: a rage click, error spike, or customer Slack message triggers a background agent that groups related errors and session replays, researches the codebase, and opens a PR — developers wake up to green PRs instead of dashboards.
- Three hard lessons from building it: off-the-shelf embedding models cluster signals by structural similarity rather than meaning, requiring custom solutions; and the pipeline must handle noisy, ambiguous signals at production scale.
- The broader implication is that product observability is becoming a target for agentic automation — not just monitoring, but autonomous remediation.
