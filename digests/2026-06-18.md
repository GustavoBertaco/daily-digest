## Daniel Pink

**[How Investors Get Big Returns](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t7qla3eGU9I)**
The video's thesis: a well-documented behavioral bias — not lack of information — explains why most investors underperform the gains available to them.

- **Name the bias.** Behavioral economists call it the disposition effect: the tendency to sell winners too early and hold losers too long.
- **It's backed by long-run data.** Shefrin and Statman documented the pattern in 1985 across thousands of real investor accounts, not a theoretical model.
- **It's the inverse of what compounding rewards.** Compounding favors letting winners run and cutting losers — the disposition effect drives investors to do exactly the opposite.
