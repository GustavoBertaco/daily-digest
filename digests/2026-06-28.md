## AI Engineer

**[Stop Writing Tone Instructions. Layer Them. - Isadora Martin-Dye, Isadora & Co](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ij-AU9dpJjc)**
Isadora Martin-Dye's thesis: a brand voice that survives real users isn't a one-time instruction but an architecture — built from production work on a wedding venue, a personal AI companion, and a tool for families of missing people, she breaks voice into four layers: immutable identity, situational mode, example-anchored voice, and a deterministic post-generation veto.

- **Voice is an architecture, not a prompt.** Writing tone instructions once and hoping they hold is the failure mode the talk targets; layering is the fix.
- **Four layers do distinct jobs.** Immutable identity, situational mode, example-anchored voice, and a deterministic post-generation veto each handle a different part of keeping voice consistent.
- **The real test is turn 21, not turn 1.** The talk frames the difference between a prompt that holds and one that breaks as a matter of knowing which job belongs to which layer.
- **Production domains ground the framework.** The four-layer model is drawn from real systems — a wedding venue, a personal AI companion, and a missing-persons-support tool — not a theoretical exercise.

Paul Iusztin and Louis-François Bouchard's thesis: turning a large personal corpus of notes, videos, documents, and repositories into usable AI context takes more than a bigger context window — it requires real memory and context engineering, organizing sources, indexing what matters, and loading only what the model needs.

- **Context window size isn't the bottleneck.** The talk explicitly positions memory and context engineering as the requirement, not simply expanding how much text a model can ingest at once.
- **Indexing and selective loading are the core mechanics.** Organizing sources and loading only what's needed are called out as the operative techniques, rather than dumping everything into context.
- **The corpus is large and heterogeneous.** The talk works from roughly 10,994 notes spanning videos, documents, and repositories, not a single content type.
- **The implementation is open and reusable.** The authors point to an open-source implementation and a related course, framing this as a reproducible workflow rather than a one-off demo.
