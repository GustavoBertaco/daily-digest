## Daniel Pink

**[The Progress Principle](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7wqztC5tFYk)**
Daniel Pink distills one of the most actionable findings in management research: the single highest-leverage thing a manager can do is remove obstacles blocking their people's forward progress.

- **The manager's primary job is obstacle removal.** The highest-leverage thing a manager can do in any given week is identify and clear one blocker that is slowing someone down.
- **Immediately actionable.** No large-scale initiative required — removing one obstacle this week is enough to shift their team's progress.

# 🤖 AI & Machine Learning

## AI Engineer

**[Building Great Agent Skills: The Missing Manual](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UNzCG3lw6O0)**
The talk tackles "skill hell" — the fragmented, inconsistent state of agent skill development — by introducing a structured checklist framework that gives developers and organizations a shared rubric for building effective, maintainable AI agent skills.

- **Trigger design is a first-class decision.** The framework treats the choice between user-invoked and model-invoked skill triggers as a deliberate architectural call, not an afterthought — getting it wrong causes skills to fire at the wrong time or be missed entirely.
- **Structure governs maintainability.** Organizing steps and references within a skill matters as much as the logic itself; poorly structured skills become brittle as agents scale.
- **A shared rubric solves the coordination problem.** Without a common checklist, teams ship skills to wildly different quality bars. The framework's value is as much organizational as technical.
- **"Skill hell" is a real production pain.** The framing names a problem practitioners recognize: without a shared rubric, developers and organizations struggle to create effective, maintainable skills for AI agents.
