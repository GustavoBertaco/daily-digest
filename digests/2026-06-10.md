## Uber Engineering

**[Apache Hudi™ at Uber: Trillion-Record-Scale Data Lake](https://www.uber.com/blog/apache-hudi-at-uber/)**
Deep dive into how Uber manages one of the world's most demanding data ecosystems using Apache Hudi. Covers the architecture behind a data lake that ingests streams from hundreds of microservices across thousands of cities, and the operational challenges of running it at trillion-record scale.

**[Multi-Cloud Secrets Management Platform](https://www.uber.com/blog/building-ubers-multi-cloud-secrets-management-platform/)**
Uber runs 5,000 microservices and manages 150,000+ secrets for authentication across its distributed systems. This post details how they built a multi-cloud secrets platform to handle 400+ third-party vendor integrations and SaaS apps — with rising cyberattack pressure as context.

**[Zero-Growth Stack: 10% CPU Savings in Go](https://www.uber.com/blog/zero-growth-stack/)**
65% of Uber's services run on Go across 2 million+ cores, so even a 1% efficiency gain is worth millions. This post explains how they achieved a 10% CPU reduction in one service by leveraging stack allocation optimizations in the Go runtime, with plans to replicate it broadly.

**[The 5 Layers Every Cloud Commitment Depends On](https://www.uber.com/blog/five-layers-cloud/)**
VP-level perspective on what a large-scale cloud commitment actually entails. Frames it as a distributed systems problem with a ten-figure price tag — the five layers being the technical domains organizations typically only discover after signing.

**[Ansible Automation for Uber's Global Corporate Network](https://www.uber.com/blog/ansible-automation-powers/)**
How Uber manages network connectivity across six continents using Ansible, serving thousands of engineers daily. Covers orchestrating both cloud and on-prem infrastructure with operational reliability as the key constraint.

**[Hybrid Core Allocation: From Overallocation to Reliable Sharing](https://www.uber.com/blog/hybrid-core-allocation/)**
Follow-up to Uber's cpusets work in Odin (their stateful container orchestration). Their vertical CPU scaler had always assumed CPUs could be over-allocated — this post explains how they moved toward hybrid allocation for better throttling reduction and latency.

**[Database Overload → Intelligent Load Management](https://www.uber.com/blog/from-static-rate-limiting-to-intelligent-load-management/)**
Uber's Docstore and Schemaless databases serve 170M+ monthly active users. Static rate-limiting was breaking down at scale. This traces the journey from hardcoded limits to an adaptive load management system.

**[High-Performance gRPC in OpenSearch](https://www.uber.com/blog/high-performance-grpc/)**
Search underlies real-time matching, fraud detection, and Uber Eats recommendations. As traffic grew, REST/JSON became a bottleneck. This details how integrating gRPC into OpenSearch unblocked further search platform scaling.

**[Validating Bounding Box Annotations with AI](https://www.uber.com/blog/validating-bounding-box/)**
Human annotation errors degrade model quality. Uber AI Solutions built a system to validate bounding box labels using expert-in-the-loop pipelines, catching errors before they reach training data.

**[Evolution of Uber's Delivery Search Platform](https://www.uber.com/blog/evolution-and-scale-of-ubers-delivery-search-platform/)**
Search is a primary discovery funnel for Uber Eats. Chronicles how their search platform evolved to handle long-tail queries, seasonal items, and multilingual markets — and what happens when it misses user intent.

## AI Engineer Conference

**[Stop Making Models Bigger, Make Them Behave — Kobie Crawford, Snorkel](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TNwJ1LMiENk)**
Snorkel's research: the biggest gains often come from better data, not larger models. Covers a specific use case in partnership with UC Berkeley's RLLM group — high-quality expert-in-the-loop data applied to the right problem delivers outsized results vs. scaling parameters.

**[Sovereign Escape Velocity: Ownership with Open Models — Google DeepMind](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SS-A8sE7hkw)**
Google DeepMind presents Gemma 4: four model sizes, two targeting mobile/IoT (E2B/E4B). The core thesis — Gemini is the most capable hosted option, but when data can't leave your infrastructure, open models are the only path. Strong framing for the proprietary vs. open-source decision.

**[Self-Driving Products: Product Signals to Pull Requests — Joshua Snyder, PostHog](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zMiSRliEzv4)**
PostHog is building a pipeline where product signals (errors, metric changes) trigger background agents that investigate root causes and open PRs automatically — skipping the dashboard entirely. Lessons: signal ingestion, agent orchestration, and safe auto-deployment behind feature flags.
