## AI Engineer

**[The Production AI Playbook: Deploying Agents at Enterprise Scale — Sandipan Bhaumik, Databricks](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ObTPqBGsEbA)**
The talk's thesis: a retail bank spent £85,000 over six months on a chatbot proof-of-concept that never reached production because no one could diagnose why it kept failing — the fix wasn't a better model, but better infrastructure around it.

- **Model selection came last, not first.** Bhaumik's team didn't pick the model until week seven of an eight-week engagement; the first six weeks went entirely to evaluation data, tracing infrastructure, and a measurement pipeline.
- **Observability is what makes production AI debuggable.** When the bank updated its interest rate policy post-launch and customer satisfaction dropped, the tracing system — not guesswork — caught the cause tied to the new policy.
- **The original PoC failed from invisibility, not capability.** £85,000 and six months were spent without anyone able to explain the failure mode, underscoring that measurement infrastructure is a prerequisite for enterprise agent deployments, not an afterthought.
