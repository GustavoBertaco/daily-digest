**[Ask These 2 Questions Before You Start](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ts4AmJS4TCw)**
*Published: June 11, 2026*

- This reframes focus as a motivation problem before it is a habit or tool problem.
- The actionable takeaway: before optimizing a system for focus, answer why the work matters — without that, any system is fighting against the grain.

**[Laugh At YOURSELF](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mKo_ql-od3g)**
*Published: June 9, 2026*

- Drawing on research from Cheng and colleagues at Stanford, Pink explains that self-deprecation reads as confidence when stakes are low and as indifference when stakes are high — the same behavior carries opposite social signals depending on context.
- The mechanism matters: laughter signals something different in high-stakes vs. low-stakes situations, so the tool's effect depends entirely on when it is used.
- The counterintuitive implication is that self-deprecating humor, often promoted as universally disarming, can actively undermine credibility in precisely the moments where credibility matters most.

**[Perspective Taking](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zQd8CadWmWw)**
*Published: June 9, 2026*

- Pink frames perspective-taking not as agreement but as accuracy — correctly modeling what another person sees and thinks, which is a learnable cognitive skill rather than an empathy exercise.
- Adam Galinsky's 2006 study illustrates the power asymmetry directly: participants primed with power drew the letter E readable to themselves; lower-power participants drew it readable to others — those with the most power practiced perspective-taking the least.
- The actionable implication is that the people in organizations who most need perspective-taking ability (leaders and decision-makers) are structurally the least likely to have practiced it.
