## Daniel Pink

**[Motivational Interviewing](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r9XYCu4IElM)**
Daniel Pink argues that durable influence flows from eliciting a person's own reasoning rather than issuing commands — the operating principle of motivational interviewing. The technique applies identically whether the conversation is about parenting, closing a sale, or leading a team.

- **Questions generate ownership, commands generate resistance.** When someone articulates their own reasons for change, they become invested in the argument. Being told what to do produces the opposite effect.
- **Self-driven change is the goal, not compliance.** Pink frames influence as strongest when it originates internally.
- **The mechanism is psychological, not situational.** Pink explicitly names parenting, sales, and leadership as contexts where this applies.
