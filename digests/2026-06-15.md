## 📺 Daniel Pink

### [Why Finishing Feels Worse Than Starting](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2zyIZV-M8mc)
*Published: June 13, 2026*

- The final stretch of a tedious task tends to feel worse than earlier stages — not because the task gets harder, but because your brain accumulates awareness of how much effort you've already endured.
- Effort awareness, not task difficulty, is the real variable: what changes at the end is your mind's running tally of what's been suffered, which amplifies perceived discomfort.
- The counterintuitive fix is to zoom out — reframing the task at a broader scale makes it appear smaller, reducing its psychological weight when you're closest to done.
