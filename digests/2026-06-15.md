# 🤖 AI & Machine Learning

## AI Engineer

**[Why MCP and ChatGPT Apps Use Double Iframes — Frédéric Barthelet, Alpic](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=c-2eEv2ou7Y)**
The thesis: the iframe-inside-an-iframe you find when inspecting ChatGPT's DOM during an MCP app render isn't over-engineering — it's the only structure left standing after every simpler approach breaks security.

- **`srcdoc` fails on origin.** An inline `srcdoc` iframe shares the parent origin, so ChatGPT's CSP blocks all third-party scripts and the app can't run.
- **Relaxing the CSP breaks isolation.** Loosening the CSP so the app can load would let any app read ChatGPT's localStorage and cookies — an unacceptable cross-app data leak.
- **`sandbox` and `allow-same-origin` collide.** Adding `sandbox` strips origin-indexed storage; adding `allow-same-origin` to restore it is the classic sandbox escape.
- **The double iframe is the residual.** Nesting one iframe inside another is what remains after ruling out every simpler option — isolation enforced by structure rather than by policy.
