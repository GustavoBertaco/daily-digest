## 📺 Daniel Pink

### [Talk to You 10 Years from Now!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jgAndKYEQnU)
*Published: June 7, 2026*

- Pink uses a thought experiment — a conversation with your future self 10 years out — to reframe the cost of inaction
- Your future self already knows what you should do: build a solid foundation, take the shot, do the right thing
- The discomfort isn't about effort; it's about explaining to that future person *why* you didn't act
- Framed as accountability to yourself across time, not external pressure

---

### [The Second Score](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U7BA8PPD4Fw)
*Published: June 7, 2026*

- After 30 years writing about human performance, Pink's key insight: there are always two scores, and most people only track one
- **First score**: the grade, the review, the evaluation — visible and obvious
- **Second score**: how you *respond* to the first score — do you get defensive, or stay open and curious?
- The second score compounds over a career; it determines whether feedback actually improves your performance

## 📺 AI Engineer

### [LLM Observability, Evaluation, Experimentation Platform — Dat Ngo, Arize](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=...)
*Published: June 7, 2026*

- Arize's platform treats LLM observability as a first-class problem: traces, evals, and experiments in one loop rather than three separate tools
- Key architectural decision: store all traces in a columnar format so you can slice by any dimension (model, prompt version, user cohort) without pre-defining dashboards
- Evaluation is tied directly to production traces rather than static test sets — drift in real traffic triggers re-evaluation automatically
- Practical takeaway: running evals on sampled production data catches regressions that curated benchmarks miss

---

### [Under 5 minutes to a deployed LLM endpoint — Audry Hsu, RunPod](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=...)
*Published: June 7, 2026*

- RunPod's new serverless endpoint API abstracts cold-start management: you define the model, they handle scaling from 0
- Billing is per-second of compute, not per-request — matters for bursty workloads where per-request pricing punishes you on warm-up time
- Compatible with vLLM and TGI out of the box; no custom container required for most HuggingFace models
- Cold start benchmarked at under 90 seconds for 7B models — acceptable for async workflows, still too slow for synchronous user-facing calls
