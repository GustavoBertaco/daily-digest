## 📺 Daniel Pink

### [Talk to You 10 Years from Now!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jgAndKYEQnU)
*Published: June 7, 2026*

Pink uses a thought experiment — a conversation with your future self, 10 years from now — to reframe the cost of inaction.

- Your future self already knows what you should do: build a solid foundation, take the shot, do the right thing
- The discomfort is not about effort; it's about explaining to that future person *why* you didn't act
- Framed as accountability to yourself across time, not external pressure
- Short clip (~1 min), likely excerpted from a longer interview or talk

### [The Second Score](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U7BA8PPD4Fw)
*Published: June 7, 2026*

After 30 years writing about human performance, Pink argues the most important thing he's learned about feedback is that there are always two scores — and most people only track one.

- **First score**: the grade, the review, the evaluation — visible and obvious
- **Second score**: how you *respond* to the first score — do you get defensive, shut down, or stay open and curious?
- The second score is the one that compounds over a career: it determines whether feedback actually improves your performance
- Pink's prescription: treat your response to criticism as a separate, scoreable performance in its own right
