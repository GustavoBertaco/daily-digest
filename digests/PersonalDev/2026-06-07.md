## 📺 Daniel Pink

### [Talk to You 10 Years from Now!](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jgAndKYEQnU)
*Published: June 7, 2026*

A short motivational frame from Pink built around a single question: could you justify your current inaction to your future self?

- The core idea: your 10-years-from-now self already knows what you *should* be doing today
- Pink frames regret as a failure of accountability to your own future — not a failure of effort
- Designed to push people off the fence on decisions they've been postponing ("take the shot, build the foundation, make the call")
- Short clip (~1-2 min) — acts as a standalone nudge, likely excerpted from a longer talk

### [The Second Score](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U7BA8PPD4Fw)
*Published: June 7, 2026*

Pink introduces a two-score model for evaluating performance that separates outcome from response.

- **First score**: the raw result — a grade, a review, a missed target
- **Second score**: how you behave *after* a bad first score — composure, learning, professionalism
- The argument: the second score is often more visible and more consequential than the first
- Practical application: useful reframe for anyone receiving negative feedback or navigating a setback at work
