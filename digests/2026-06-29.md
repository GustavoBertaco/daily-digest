## Daniel Pink

**[It's Your Fault](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=y49oYPhJ6ls)**
Daniel Pink's thesis: regret is proof of agency, not proof that something is wrong with you — the feeling itself is information to act on rather than a verdict to absorb.

- **Regret requires agency.** You can't regret someone else's choice — the sting of regret is, by definition, tied to decisions that were actually yours to make.
- **The feeling is data, not a diagnosis.** Pink reframes regret away from "something is wrong with me" and toward a signal pointing at what to examine.
- **Unpacking beats suppressing.** Citing Mel Robbins, the video frames the move as actively "unpacking the suitcase" to find what's weighing you down, rather than carrying it unexamined.
